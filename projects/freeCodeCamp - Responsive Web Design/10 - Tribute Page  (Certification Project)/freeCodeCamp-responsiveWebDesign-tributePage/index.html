Sociedade Esportiva Palmeiras

The pride of Brazilian football

Here's a timeline of Sociedade Esportiva Palmeiras' history:

  • 1914 - Founded on August 26 as Palestra Italia by Italian immigrants in São Paulo
  • 1915 - Plays its first official match, winning 2-0 against Savoia
  • 1920 - Wins its first Paulista Championship, starting its journey as one of Brazil's top football clubs
  • 1942 - Changes its name to Sociedade Esportiva Palmeiras due to World War II
  • 1951 - Wins the Copa Rio, regarded as the first international club competition
  • 1965 - Celebrates its golden era, winning several state and national championships
  • 1993 - Wins the Campeonato Brasileiro Série A, marking the return to glory days
  • 1999 - Wins the Copa Libertadores, the most prestigious club competition in South American football
  • 2014 - Opens Allianz Parque, a modern stadium built on the site of the old Palestra Italia Stadium
  • 2016 - Wins its ninth Campeonato Brasileiro Série A title
  • 2018 - Wins its tenth Campeonato Brasileiro Série A title
  • 2020 - Wins its second Copa Libertadores title and its fourth Copa do Brasil title
  • 2021 - Wins its third Copa Libertadores title, solidifying its place among the football elite
  • 2022 - Wins its eleventh Campeonato Brasileiro Série A title
  • 2023 - Wins its Twelfth Campeonato Brasileiro Série A title

"Palmeiras is not just a football club; it's a symbol of passion, tradition, and excellence in Brazilian sports."

-- Brazilian Football Confederation
